# Morning Dinacharya: The Classical Daily Ritual

**Anchor:** First 30–45 min after waking (before food)
**Goal:** Kapha clearance, agni (digestive fire) stimulation, ojas building

## Sequence
1. **Jihwa nirlekhana** — Tongue scraping with copper scraper (7 strokes, root to tip)
2. **Kavala/Gandusha** — Oil pulling with sesame oil, 5 min (swirl, do NOT swallow, spit in bin)
3. **Warm water + lemon** — 200 ml; add 20 ml Amla juice OR 1 tsp Chyawanprash
4. **Shilajit** — 250 mg resin dissolved in warm milk (not water; avoid with iron supplementation timing)
5. **Tulsi tea** — 2–3 leaves steeped in hot water OR Triphala-water 1 tsp if constipation present

Order is non-negotiable: clear → cleanse → nourish.

## Why (Ayurvedic Rationale)
Classical Ayurveda (Ashtanga Hridayam, Dinacharya chapter) mandates clearing ama (metabolic waste) from tongue/mouth first — unprocessed kapha accumulates overnight. Oil pulling draws lipophilic toxins from oral mucosa. Warm lemon + Amla stimulates jatharagni (gastric fire). Shilajit classified as yogavahi (enhances whatever it is combined with) — warm milk activates anabolic channel.

## Cautions
- Sesame oil pulling: do not swallow; spit in dustbin (not sink — blocks drain)
- Shilajit: avoid during active infections/fever; consult for haemochromatosis (iron overload)
- Chyawanprash contains sugar — diabetics use sugar-free variant (varies by brand)
- Triphala laxative effect dose-dependent; start ½ tsp
