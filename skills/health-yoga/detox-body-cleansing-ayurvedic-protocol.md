# Detox & Body Cleansing (Liver, Blood, Lymph) — Ayurvedic Protocol

**Goal slug:** detox
**Indian-context relevance:** Urban Indians face high toxic load from pollution, processed food, and sedentary lifestyles — Ayurvedic detox (shodhana) targets liver, blood, and lymphatic channels (srotas).

## Primary Ingredients
- **Triphala** (1:1:1 ratio, Charaka) — tri-doshic deepaniya; 1 tsp churna promotes colon-based ama elimination
- **Neem** (Azadirachta indica) — tikta herb for blood/liver detox; 1 capsule 250mg AM
- **Manjistha** (Rubia cordifolia) — rakta-shodhaka; 500mg cap for lymphatic/blood cleansing
- **Guduchi/Giloy** (Tinospora cordifolia) — immunomodulator + hepatoprotective; limit to <3 months; 300mg extract

## Supporting Ingredients
- **Amla** (Emblica officinalis) — vitamin C antioxidant; 20ml juice AM
- **Haldi** (Curcuma longa) — hepatoprotective via curcumin 500mg + piperine

## Daily Regimen
- **Morning (empty stomach):** Amla juice 20ml + Neem capsule 250mg with warm water
- **Afternoon:** Haldi-piperine 500mg with food (fat required for absorption)
- **Bedtime:** Triphala churna 1 tsp in warm water; Manjistha 500mg

## Timeline & Expectations
- **Week 1–2:** Bowel regularity improves; skin may briefly break out (ama mobilisation)
- **Week 4:** Skin clarity improves; energy lifts
- **Week 6:** Full detox cycle; check LFTs if on Giloy

## Lifestyle Anchors (Charaka non-negotiables)
- Avoid maida, deep-fried, and alcohol during detox — they reload ama faster than herbs clear it
- 8–10 glasses warm (not cold) water daily supports lymphatic flow
- Light exercise (walking 30 min) activates svedana channels essential for detox

## Cautions for This Goal
- **Giloy HILI warning:** Tinospora cordifolia linked to herb-induced liver injury (HILI) — Kulkarni 2022 Hepatol Commun, Nagral 2021/2023 J Clin Exp Hepatol. Do NOT exceed 3 months continuous use. Monitor LFTs. Avoid in pre-existing liver disease.
- Triphala may cause loose stools initially; reduce dose if so
- Neem contraindicated in pregnancy and in children <12
