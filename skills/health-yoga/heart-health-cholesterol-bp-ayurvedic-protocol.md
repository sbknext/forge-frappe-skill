# Heart Health, Cholesterol & BP — Ayurvedic Protocol

**Goal slug:** heart-health
**Indian-context relevance:** Indians carry 2–4× genetic CVD risk vs global average; early lipid dysregulation common from age 30 — Ayurveda supports cardiometabolic health alongside lifestyle.

## Primary Ingredients
- **Arjuna** (Terminalia arjuna) — hridaya (cardiac) rasayana; bark decoction/kshira paka reduces LDL, improves cardiac output; 1 tsp bark powder with milk
- **Haldi + Piperine** — curcumin 500mg + black pepper 5mg; anti-inflammatory + LDL-lowering
- **Amla** — antioxidant; inhibits platelet aggregation; 20ml juice AM

## Supporting Ingredients
- **Triphala** — lipid metabolism via amalaki; 1 tsp bedtime
- **Tulsi** — adaptogen; reduces stress-driven cortisol-HTN cycle
- **Ashwagandha** (KSM-66) — stress-driven hypertension; 300mg BID
- **Shilajit** — mitochondrial support; 250mg AM

## Daily Regimen
- **Morning (empty stomach):** Amla juice 20ml + Arjuna kshira (1 tsp bark boiled in 200ml milk)
- **With breakfast:** Haldi-piperine 500mg
- **Evening:** Ashwagandha 300mg (if stress-driven HTN)
- **Bedtime:** Triphala 1 tsp warm water; Shilajit 250mg in warm milk

## Timeline & Expectations
- **Week 4:** Energy and exertion tolerance improve
- **Week 8:** Lipid panel improvement (LDL ↓, HDL ↑); BP trending lower
- **Week 12:** Measurable lipid improvement if diet compliant

## Lifestyle Anchors (Charaka non-negotiables)
- Reduce refined carbs and trans fat — Arjuna cannot compensate for 3 samosas/day
- 40 min brisk walk 5×/wk — activates vyana vayu, the cardiac vayu
- Seasonal fruit (especially amla season Oct–Jan) as daily ritual

## Cautions for This Goal
- Arjuna bark lowers BP — monitor if on antihypertensives; may need dose adjustment
- Shilajit: buy from reputed brand (verify fulvic acid 15–60% COA); fulvic acid 15–60%; avoid >70% claims
- KSM-66 Ashwagandha + thyroid medication — monitor TSH at 4 weeks (can lower T4 dose requirement)
- These herbs are adjuncts to statins/antihypertensives, NOT replacements
