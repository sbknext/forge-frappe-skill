# Amla (Amalaki) — *Phyllanthus emblica*

**Sanskrit/Regional:** Amalaki, Dhatri ("nurse/mother"), Amrita, Divyaphala; Nelli (Tamil), Usirikai (Telugu), Avla (Marathi)
**Active compounds:** Emblicanin A & B, punigluconin, pedunculagin, gallic acid, ellagic acid, ascorbic acid (~600–900mg/100g, **heat-stable due to tannin complexation**)

> Translucent green-yellow sour fruit; foremost Rasayana per Charaka Samhita. Richest practical natural Vitamin C source in Indian tradition.

## Dosage
- **Powder:** 3–6g/day with warm water
- **Juice:** 20ml diluted in 100ml water — morning empty stomach
- **Capsules:** 500mg × 2/day
- **Murabba (preserve):** 1 piece/day classical maintenance dose

## Ayurvedic Properties
- **Rasa:** Pancharasa — five tastes (all except Lavana/salty); predominant Amla (sour)
- **Guna:** Laghu (light), Ruksha (dry)
- **Virya:** **Sheeta (cold)** — exception among sour items; most sours are Ushna
- **Vipaka:** Madhura
- **Dosha:** Tridoshic; especially Pitta-shamak
- **Karma:** Rasayana (rejuvenative), Chakshushya (eye), Hridya (cardiac), Keshya (hair), Vrishya (aphrodisiac), Medhya
- **Classical:** Ashtanga Hridayam calls Amalaki "foremost among all Rasayanas"; Charaka lists it as head of rejuvenatives

## Health Benefits
- **Vitamin C/Immunity:** heat-stable tannin-complexed ascorbic acid (unlike citrus Vit C); survives cooking in Chyawanprash
- **Hair:** Keshya — anti-premature greying, anti-fall, hair growth; Amla oil with Bhringaraj = gold standard
- **Skin:** anti-aging Pitta-cooling, glow, collagen support
- **Digestion:** despite sourness, Sheeta virya cools Pitta — anti-hyperacidity, anti-GERD adjunct
- **Diabetes:** insulin sensitizing via gallic/ellagic acid; Nishamalaki classical formula
- **Lipid/cardio:** emblicanins reduce LDL oxidation; cardioprotective studies
- **Liver:** hepatoprotective vs toxin-induced damage (animal studies)
- **Eye health:** Chakshushya — antioxidant protection; traditional eye-wash Triphala (Amla primary)
- **Cancer chemoprevention:** ellagic acid + emblicanins; preclinical

## Common Preparations
- **Chyawanprash:** Amla = primary base (~5.34g fresh fruit equivalent per serving Patanjali/Dabur) — the iconic winter Rasayana
- **Amla murabba:** whole fruit preserve in jaggery; classical Rasayana form
- **Juice:** warm water dawn — most bioavailable
- **Triphala:** 1/3 ingredient = Pitta component
- **Nishamalaki:** Amla + Haldi powder — classical diabetes management formula
- **Amla-Bhringaraj hair oil:** Keshya classical combination
- **Dhatri lauh:** Amla + iron powder = classical iron-deficiency preparation

## Sourcing
Pratapgarh UP = primary premium GI-zone for processing Amla (Chakaiya/NA varieties); Salem TN for BSR-1 south processing. Several D2C brands (Kapiva) tout Pratapgarh-origin. Buy cold-pressed juice or whole fruit when possible.

## Best Paired With

- **Acidity:** Mulethi (Yashtimadhu) + Amla
- **Classical formulas:** Chyawanprash, Triphala, Brahma Rasayana, Amalaki Rasayana, Dhatri lauh, Kushmandavaleha

## Contraindications
- **Cold/cough/loose stools:** Sheeta virya may aggravate — avoid in acute cold episodes
- **Hyperkalemia:** potassium-rich; caution in CKD/potassium-sparing diuretics
- **Anticoagulants:** mild antiplatelet (Vit C + tannins); separate from warfarin timing
- **Hypoglycemics:** additive; monitor
- **Recent surgery:** stop 2 weeks pre-op (antiplatelet)
- **Dental erosion:** high-acid fresh juice; rinse mouth after; don't brush immediately

## Adulteration Markers & Certifications
- Powder cut with acidic fillers (tamarind, hibiscus powder, citric acid + bulking agents)
- Juice adulterated with water + citric acid + green food coloring
- Look for: cold-pressed (no heating), no preservatives, Vitamin C content lab-disclosed, Pratapgarh-origin with batch traceability
- Certs: FSSAI, GMP, NPOP organic, AGMARK (for dried)
