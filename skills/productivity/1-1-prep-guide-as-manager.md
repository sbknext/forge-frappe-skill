Prepare for your 1:1 with a direct report to make it growth-oriented, not a status meeting.

## When to use
10 minutes before your 1:1 with each direct report.

## Mindset
Your job in a 1:1 is to unblock, develop, and listen — not to receive status. If status is all you're getting, the culture of the meeting needs to change.

## Template
```markdown
## 1:1 Manager Prep — [Report Name] — [Date]

### What I know about their context this week
- [Project status in one sentence]
- [Any signals from Slack/PRs/meetings that suggest friction or progress?]

### What I want to ask (pick 2–3)
- What's most frustrating you right now?
- What are you most proud of this week/month?
- Is there anything I'm doing (or not doing) that makes your work harder?
- What would help you most right now — more autonomy, more support, or more context?
- What are you working on for your own growth?

### Feedback I want to give
- [Specific, recent, positive]
- [Specific, recent, developmental — only if timely and actionable]

### My open asks
- [Action item or decision I need from them]

### Long-term career track
- [Where do they want to go? What's the next step I'm helping create?]
```

## Tips
- Rotate through question sets. Asking "what's frustrating you" every week creates diminishing returns.
- If they bring the agenda (see direct-report template), follow their agenda. It's a sign of ownership — reinforce it.
- After difficult feedback, check in specifically at the next 1:1: "How are you feeling about what we discussed last week?"
