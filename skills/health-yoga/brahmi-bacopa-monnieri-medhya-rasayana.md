# Brahmi (*Bacopa monnieri*) — Medhya Rasayana

**Sanskrit/Regional:** Brahmi, Saraswati ("Goddess of learning"), Jalanimba, Aindri; Neer Brahmi (Tamil), Jala Brahmi (Telugu)
**Active compounds:** Bacosides A & B, bacopasaponins I–X, bacopasides, alkaloids (brahmine, herpestine)

> ℹ️ **Species note — critical disambiguation:**
> **Brahmi = *Bacopa monnieri*** per Charaka, Sushruta, Vagbhata, Kerala/South tradition, and Ayurvedic Formulary of India.
> **Centella asiatica = Mandukaparni** (frog-leaf plant; Apiaceae family; Bhavaprakasha 16th c. conflated the two).
> These are pharmacologically distinct: Bacopa = stimulant nootropic, memory, attention; Centella = nervine restorative, skin healing, calming.
> North India commonly sells Centella as "Brahmi" — always verify Latin species on label. Echo Shop explicitly declares *Bacopa monnieri*.

## Dosage
- **Standardized extract (≥20% bacosides):** 300–450mg/day
- **Raw powder:** 3–6g/day with warm milk + ghee
- **Minimum effective duration:** 8–12 weeks (NOT acute effect herb)

## Ayurvedic Properties
- **Rasa:** Tikta (bitter), Kashaya (astringent), Madhura (sweet)
- **Guna:** Laghu (light), Sara (mobile)
- **Virya:** Sheeta (cool)
- **Vipaka:** Madhura
- **Dosha:** Tridoshic; primarily Pitta-Vata shamak
- **Karma:** Medhya (intellect), Smritivardhaka (memory enhancer), Ayushya (longevity), Rasayana, Prajasthapana
- **Classical refs:** Charaka Sutrasthana Medhya Rasayanas (four principal herbs); Sushruta Samhita

## Health Benefits
- **Memory/learning/recall:** Roodenrys 2002, Stough 2001 — 12-week RCTs show improved word recall, processing speed, and attention (bacosides mechanism: antioxidant + acetylcholinesterase modulation)
- **Anxiety:** comparable to lorazepam in preclinical; human RCTs confirm reduced anxiety scores
- **ADHD adjunct:** Negi 2000 trial; combined attention + hyperactivity improvement
- **Epilepsy/Apasmara:** classical indication; GABA-modulatory preclinical
- **Anti-aging cognition:** neuroprotection via antioxidant cascade
- **Hair growth:** Brahmi oil topical — promotes hair follicle growth preclinical
- **Sleep:** mild sedative at higher doses
- **Antioxidant neuroprotection:** reduces β-amyloid aggregation (Alzheimer's preclinical)

## Common Preparations
- **Brahmi ghrita:** ghee preparation — classical night formula with warm milk
- **Brahmi taila:** scalp oil for cognitive + hair benefits
- **Kashayam:** decoction
- **Anxiety stack:** Brahmi + Shankhpushpi + Jatamansi
- **Cognitive stack:** Brahmi + Vacha (Acorus calamus) + Shankhpushpi

## Sourcing
Wetland herb — Kerala, West Bengal, Bihar, Odisha; mostly wild-harvested from marshy areas. Limited commercial cultivation. Wetland habitat loss threatens supply.

## Best Paired With
- **Medhya quartet:** Shankhpushpi + Jatamansi + Vacha
- **Absorption:** ghee + warm milk (fat-soluble bacosides)
- **Anxiety stack:** Brahmi + Ashwagandha + Jatamansi
- **Classical formulas:** Brahmi ghrita, Saraswatarishta, Brahmi vati, Saraswata churna

## Contraindications
- **Bradycardia/slow heart rate:** Bacopa may further slow heart rate
- **Thyroid medication:** may increase T4 — monitor if on levothyroxine
- **GI cramping/nausea:** common at high doses; take with food
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** safety not established — avoid
- **Acute mental agitation (high Vata):** start low; Sheeta virya can increase subtle Vata initially

## Adulteration Markers & Certifications
- **Most common fraud:** Centella asiatica sold as "Brahmi"
- **Verification:** Label must say *Bacopa monnieri* (Latin), not just "Brahmi"
- **COA:** demand bacoside % standardization
- **Look for:** AYUSH license, GMP, species-authenticated
