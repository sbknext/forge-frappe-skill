# Tulsi (Holy Basil) — *Ocimum tenuiflorum*

**Sanskrit/Regional:** Tulasi, Surasa, Vishnupriya, Brinda; Trittavu (Malayalam), Thulasi (Tamil/Kannada), Vrinda (Bengali)
**Active compounds:** Eugenol, ursolic acid, rosmarinic acid, linalool, β-caryophyllene, apigenin, luteolin, ocimumosides A&B (adaptogens)

## Varieties / Grades

| Variety | Species | Eugenol | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rama (Sri) | *O. tenuiflorum* | ~5.6 mg/g | Green, mild — everyday/temple use |
| Krishna (Shyama) | *O. tenuiflorum* | Highest of species | Purple-tinged, sharp peppery — **best respiratory** |
| Vana | *O. gratissimum* | ~8.89 mg/g | Woody tree-basil; lemony-clove; highest overall eugenol |
| Amrita | *O. tenuiflorum* cultivar | — | Perennial high-yield cultivation |
| Kapoor | Often *O. africanum* | Low | Often mislabeled; pharmacologically distinct |

## Dosage
- **Fresh leaves:** 2–4 leaves/day chewed or in water
- **Dried capsule:** 300–600mg/day
- **Drops concentrate:** 10–15 drops in water 2x/day
- **Tea/kadha:** 1–2 cups daily

## Ayurvedic Properties
- **Rasa:** Katu (pungent), Tikta (bitter)
- **Guna:** Laghu (light), Ruksha (dry)
- **Virya:** Ushna (hot)
- **Vipaka:** Katu
- **Dosha:** Kapha-Vata shamak; mild Pitta-aggravating at high doses
- **Karma:** Kasahara (anti-tussive), Jwaraghna (antipyretic), Hridya (cardiac tonic), Medhya (cognitive), Krimighna (antimicrobial)
- **Classical refs:** Charaka Sutrasthana; Bhavaprakasha Pushpavarga

## Health Benefits
- **Respiratory:** cough, asthma, bronchitis — Krishna variety highest; eugenol bronchodilation
- **Antiviral/immunity:** immunomodulator; COVID adjunct studied; anti-influenza preclinical
- **Adaptogen:** ocimumosides reduce cortisol; OB₁/OB₂ normalize stress hormones
- **Glycemic:** mild blood sugar modulation (ursolic acid mechanism)
- **Oral health:** antibacterial mouthwash lab evidence; datun substitute
- **Skin:** topical acne, ringworm (antifungal eugenol)
- **Insect repellent:** eugenol-rich Vana variety most effective
- **Cardioprotective:** lipid + BP modulation (small RCTs)
- **Fever:** antipyretic in viral fevers, classical Jwaraghna

## Common Preparations
- **Kadha:** tulsi + adrak + kali mirch + honey — classic cold/flu
- **Tulsi-Arjuna-cardamom decoction:** cardiac tonic
- **Steam inhalation:** tulsi leaves in hot water for congestion
- **Tulsi-honey paste:** topical cough/sore throat
- **Nasya drops:** tulsi + ghee in nostrils for respiratory support

## Sourcing
Pan-Indian household plant; UP/MP/Karnataka for commercial cultivation. Organic India sources from Bundelkhand smallholder network. Grow Krishna variety at home for therapeutic use.

## Best Paired With
- **Immunity kadha:** ginger + pepper + honey
- **Respiratory:** Mulethi (licorice) for soothing
- **Fever/immunity:** Giloy + Tulsi combination
- **Cardiac:** Arjuna + Tulsi
- **Classical formulas:** Tribhuvankirti rasa, Sitopaladi churna, Dashamoola combinations

## Contraindications
- **Pregnancy:** uterotonic properties — avoid concentrated extracts
- **Bleeding disorders/anticoagulants:** eugenol antiplatelet effect; stop 2 weeks pre-surgery
- **Hypothyroidism:** T4 reduction reported in some studies — monitor if on levothyroxine
- **Male fertility:** sperm count reduction in animal studies (high/chronic dose concern)
- **Pitta-dominant constitution:** Ushna virya — moderate dose; avoid in acid reflux/ulcer

## Adulteration Markers & Certifications
- Lemon basil (*O. citriodorum*) and common sweet basil often sold as Tulsi
- Vana Tulsi (O. gratissimum) sometimes undisclosed species substitution
- Demand: variety name on label (Rama/Krishna/Vana), single-species, NPOP organic, AYUSH licensed
