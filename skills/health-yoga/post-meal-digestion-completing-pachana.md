# Post-Meal Digestion: Completing Pachana

**Anchor:** Immediately after meals
**Goal:** Pachana (digestion completion) — prevent ama formation, reduce bloating

## Sequence
1. **Saunf-mishri** — ½ tsp fennel seeds + ½ tsp mishri (unrefined rock sugar); chew after meal (mukhwas)
2. **OR CCF tea** — ¼ tsp each cumin (jeera) + coriander seeds (dhania) + fennel (saunf); steep 5 min in hot water
3. **5-min walk** — 100 steps (shatapavali per classical texts)
4. **Vajrasana 5 min** (optional) — only yoga posture recommended immediately post-meal; increases blood flow to digestive organs

Do NOT lie down for 30–45 min post-meal; do NOT exercise vigorously for 2 hours.

## Why (Ayurvedic Rationale)
Charaka Samhita recommends walking 100 steps post-meal for longevity. Fennel, cumin, coriander (CCF) are tri-doshic carminatives — balanced for all body types, unlike hing which is mainly vata. Mishri (rock sugar, not white sugar) is considered laghu (light) and aids digestion without dampening agni. Vajrasana (thunderbolt pose) specifically diverts blood toward pelvic/abdominal region.

## Ingredients Used (with Indian MRP anchors)
- **Saunf (fennel seeds)** — ₹80–120/kg (any market); buy whole seeds not pre-mixed mukhwas (added sugar/colour)
- **Mishri (rock sugar)** — Mid tier ₹80/500g (varies by brand); look for uncoloured white/pale amber pieces
- **CCF tea mix** — pre-blended ₹180/25 sachets (varies by brand); or buy whole seeds individually ₹40–60 each

## Cautions
- CCF tea safe for all; saunf estrogenic in very large amounts — moderate consumption fine
- Avoid Hingvashtak or Trikatu post-meal (deepana herbs are pre-meal only)
- Vajrasana contraindicated in acute knee injury/arthritis
