# Monsoon Ritual: Immunity During Vata-Pitta Accumulation

**Anchor:** June–September (Varsha ritu); also applicable during seasonal flu/cold spikes
**Goal:** Infection prevention, digestive fire maintenance, skin protection

## Sequence (Daily)
1. **Tulsi-ginger-pepper kadha** — 3 Tulsi leaves + 1 cm fresh ginger + 3 black peppercorns boiled in 250 ml water, reduce to 150 ml; drink warm morning
2. **Haldi capsule or ¼ tsp in warm water** — anti-inflammatory baseline during monsoon infections
3. **Neem 1 capsule** — skin and infection prevention; 250 mg after meals (varies by brand)
4. **Giloy juice** — ONLY short-term, maximum 2 weeks continuous; see caution box below
5. **Avoid raw salads, cold foods, raw sprouts** — digestive fire weakened in monsoon (classical Vata provocation)

> **⚠ GILOY HILI CAUTION**
> Giloy (*Tinospora cordifolia*) has been associated with herb-induced liver injury (HILI).
> Multiple peer-reviewed case series from India:
> - Kulkarni et al. 2022, *Hepatology Communications* — multicenter India, acute hepatitis cases
> - Nagral et al. 2021, 2023, *Journal of Clinical & Experimental Hepatology*
> Several Indian marketplaces label Giloy products "100% safe" or "no side effects" — this is inaccurate.
> **Rule:** Use Giloy max 2 weeks continuously; stop immediately if jaundice, dark urine, abdominal pain, fatigue appear; get LFT before and after if using beyond 1 week. Pregnant/nursing women and those with pre-existing liver disease should avoid entirely.
> Also note: *T. crispa* (similar-looking species, hepatotoxic) is sold as Giloy in some markets — demand botanical authentication certificate.

## Why (Ayurvedic Rationale)
Varsha (monsoon) = Vata vitiation + Pitta accumulation from summer; digestive fire weakens due to humidity. Classical texts recommend laghu (light) foods, boiled water, and immunity herbs. Tulsi (*Ocimum tenuiflorum*) is Rasayana for respiratory immunity — eugenol, rosmarinic acid, ursolic acid. Neem classically used as kushthaghna (skin disease preventive) and jwaraghna (fever-preventive).

## Cautions
- Giloy HILI: see caution box above — 2-week max, LFT monitoring, stop at any liver symptoms
- Neem: avoid long-term high doses; hepatotoxic in very large quantities; do not give to children under 12
- Tulsi: avoid large medicinal doses in pregnancy (emmenagogue effect); culinary quantity safe
- Raw foods (salads, curd at night) increase ama in monsoon per classical texts — cooked food preferred
