A lightweight system to maintain meaningful relationships without relying on memory or algorithms.

## When to use
For relationships that matter but where contact is irregular — old colleagues, mentors, distant family, friends in other cities.

## Steps
1. Make a list of people you want to keep in touch with (aim for 20–40 names).
2. Assign each a cadence: Monthly, Quarterly, or Annual.
3. Add each as a recurring reminder in your calendar/todo with the person's name and a note about context.
4. When the reminder fires, send a message — it doesn't have to be long. A voice note or a relevant meme counts.
5. After each interaction, log a one-line note: date + what you talked about.

## Template (spreadsheet or Notion table)
```
| Name | Category | Cadence | Last contact | Next due | Notes |
|------|----------|---------|--------------|----------|-------|
| Rohit M | ex-colleague | Quarterly | 2026-03-10 | 2026-06-10 | Joined new startup, ask how it's going |
| Priya A | college friend | Monthly | 2026-05-01 | 2026-06-01 | Moving to Pune soon |
| Uncle Suresh | family | Annual | 2026-01-01 | 2027-01-01 | Call on his birthday |
```

## Tips
- "Staying in touch" does not require a long call. A WhatsApp voice note takes 45 seconds.
- The log note is crucial — it makes the next contact feel personal, not like a CRM ping.
- Prune ruthlessly every 6 months — if a name feels like an obligation rather than genuine care, move it to Annual or remove it.
