# Digestion, Gut Health & Acidity — Ayurvedic Protocol

**Goal slug:** digestion
**Indian-context relevance:** 60% of urban Indians report weekly digestive complaints; GERD prevalence at 8–30%; Ayurveda's agni-based GI framework offers condition-specific protocols.

## Primary Ingredients
- **Triphala** (1:1:1, Charaka) — tridoshic deepana-pachana; constipation relief; 1 tsp warm water bedtime
- **Amla** — acid-balancing pitta-shamaka; 20ml juice + Shatavari for GERD
- **Shatavari** (Asparagus racemosus) — gastric mucosal healing; 500mg with milk for gastritis/GERD

## Supporting Ingredients
- **Hingvashtak Churna** — post-meal digestive for bloating/gas; ½ tsp with ghee
- **Haldi** — gut anti-inflammatory; 500mg + piperine
- **Saunf** (fennel) + **Ajwain** (carom) — carminatives; chew post-meal
- **Yashtimadhu** (Glycyrrhiza glabra) — H. pylori inhibitor; mucosal healing with Amla+Shatavari

## Daily Regimen
- **Morning (empty stomach):** Amla juice 20ml (pitta/acidity); Triphala previous night sets AM rhythm
- **Pre-meal (10 min before):** Saunf + rock salt in warm water (stimulates agni)
- **Post-meal:** Chew saunf + ajwain; Hingvashtak 1 pinch for gas
- **Bedtime:** Triphala 1 tsp warm water for constipation; Shatavari 500mg in milk for GERD

## Timeline & Expectations
- **Week 1–2:** Bowel regularity; bloating reduces
- **Week 2–4:** Acid reflux episodes decrease
- **Week 4–6:** Gut mucosal healing; food sensitivities reduce
- **Week 8–12:** Full gut microbiome shift if diet compliant

## Lifestyle Anchors (Charaka non-negotiables)
- Eat at fixed times (dinacharya); irregular meal timing is #1 agni-disruption factor
- Avoid ice-cold water with meals — extinguishes digestive fire (agni)
- Walk 10 min after dinner (shata pavi) — reduces postprandial glucose spike and improves motility

## Cautions for This Goal
- Triphala: loose stool at high dose; start with ½ tsp and titrate up
- Yashtimadhu (licorice): avoid >4 weeks continuous; raises BP and causes hypokalemia at high dose
- Shatavari: contraindicated in oestrogen-sensitive conditions
- Hingvashtak contains asafoetida — avoid in bleeding disorders and early pregnancy
