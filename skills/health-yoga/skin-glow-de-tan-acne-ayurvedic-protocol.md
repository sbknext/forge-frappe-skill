# Skin Glow, De-tan & Acne — Ayurvedic Protocol

**Goal slug:** skin-glow
**Indian-context relevance:** India's tropical sun, pollution, and high pitta constitution make skin issues endemic — Ayurveda addresses skin from inside (rakta-shodhana) and outside (ubtan/lepa).

## Primary Ingredients
- **Haldi** (Curcuma longa) — anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial; oral 500mg curcumin + piperine; topical ubtan 2×/wk
- **Neem** (Azadirachta indica) — anti-acne; 250mg capsule AM; topical face-wash
- **Manjistha** (Rubia cordifolia) — blood purifier; 500mg cap for acne and pigmentation

## Supporting Ingredients
- **Amla** — vitamin C precursor for collagen; 20ml juice AM
- **Triphala** — gut-skin axis; 1 tsp bedtime
- **Sariva** (Hemidesmus indicus) — cooling blood purifier
- **Tulsi** — antimicrobial topical toner; Krishna variety preferred

## Daily Regimen
- **Morning:** Amla juice 20ml + Neem cap 250mg (empty stomach)
- **Afternoon:** Haldi-piperine 500mg with food + fat
- **Bedtime:** Triphala 1 tsp warm water; Manjistha 500mg
- **2×/week:** Haldi-besan ubtan (1 tsp haldi + 2 tsp besan + rose water); leave 15 min, scrub gently

## Timeline & Expectations
- **Week 2–4:** Tan reduction visible; skin tone evens
- **Week 4–8:** Glow improvement; oiliness reduces
- **Week 8–12:** Acne frequency and severity reduce
- Hormonal acne requires concurrent gut/hormonal protocol

## Lifestyle Anchors (Charaka non-negotiables)
- Avoid spicy-oily food and fermented items during active acne — aggravate pitta/kapha
- Sleep 10pm–6am; cortisol spikes at midnight worsen sebum production
- Wash face twice daily, avoid over-cleansing (disrupts skin microbiome)

## Cautions for This Goal
- Haldi stains clothing and nails; use old towels for ubtan
- Neem contraindicated in pregnancy
- Manjistha may cause urine to turn reddish — normal, not alarming
- In severe cystic acne, consult dermatologist; Ayurveda adjunct, not primary
