# Before Sleep: Nidra Ritual for Vata Pacification

**Anchor:** 30–45 min before sleep (ideally before 10 PM)
**Goal:** Tarpana (nourishment), vata pacification, deep ojas-building sleep

## Sequence
1. **Haldi-doodh (Golden Milk)** — 200 ml warm milk + ¼ tsp turmeric + pinch black pepper + 1 tsp ghee + optional: 1 tsp honey (added after cooling below 40°C, not hot)
2. **Brahmi ghrita 1 tsp** in warm milk (alternative to plain ghee) — for mental fatigue, anxiety, screen-heavy days
3. **Ashwagandha KSM-66 300 mg** — capsule with the warm milk
4. **Triphala 1 tsp** in warm water (only if sluggish bowels; not routine for everyone)
5. **Paada abhyanga** — foot massage with Bhringaraj oil or Brahmi oil (Bacopa monnieri-infused); 3–5 min each foot

## Why (Ayurvedic Rationale)
Vata dominates night (Vata time = 2–6 AM). Pre-sleep ritual pacifies this. Warm milk is tarpana — nourishes shukra (reproductive tissue) and ojas (vital essence). Ghee is the classical medhya (brain tonic) vehicle. Ashwagandha reduces sleep latency (Langade 2019, *Cureus* — 300 mg KSM-66 twice daily improved sleep quality significantly). Paada abhyanga stimulates marma points on soles connected to eye and brain channels (Ashtanga Hridayam).

## Cautions
- Never add honey to hot milk (above 40°C becomes amavisha per classical texts — controversial but prudent)
- Triphala is a gentle laxative — morning loose stools expected if using nightly; reduce dose
- Brahmi ghrita: not for acute cold/kapha congestion (increases heaviness)
- Ashwagandha: avoid in hyperthyroidism without medical supervision
