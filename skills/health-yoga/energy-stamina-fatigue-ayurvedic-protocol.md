# Energy, Stamina & Fatigue — Ayurvedic Protocol

**Goal slug:** energy
**Indian-context relevance:** Chronic fatigue is India's silent epidemic — iron deficiency, heat, and overwork deplete ojas; Shilajit + Ashwagandha are India's classical performance stack.

## Primary Ingredients
- **Shilajit** (purified resin, 15–60% fulvic acid) — mitochondrial ATP synthesis via CoQ10 enhancement; 250–500mg/d in milk
- **Ashwagandha** (KSM-66) — VO2max improvement (Choudhary 2015); 300–600mg/d
- **Chyawanprash** (Amla-base classical formula) — 49-herb rasayana; 1 tsp AM; centuries of use

## Supporting Ingredients
- **Amla** — antioxidant; iron absorption enhancer
- **Safed Musli** (Chlorophytum borivilianum) — adaptogen; 500mg for physical stamina
- **Giloy** (short-term only) — immunomodulatory energy boost

## Daily Regimen
- **Morning (empty stomach):** Shilajit pea-size (250mg) dissolved in warm milk + Chyawanprash 1 tsp
- **Post-breakfast:** Ashwagandha KSM-66 300mg; Safed Musli 500mg
- **Pre-workout (if applicable):** Ashwagandha second dose 300mg 1h before
- **Bedtime:** Chyawanprash 1 tsp (second dose in winter/high exertion)

## Timeline & Expectations
- **Week 1–2:** Sleep quality improves; morning freshness increases
- **Week 2–4:** Sustained energy through day; afternoon slump reduces
- **Week 4–8:** Stamina and workout recovery improve

## Lifestyle Anchors (Charaka non-negotiables)
- Iron-rich diet mandatory — herbs cannot compensate frank anaemia (Hb <10)
- Consistent sleep 7–8h; fatigue compounds with sleep debt faster than herbs reverse it
- Sun exposure 15–20 min AM (vitamin D) — D deficiency is fatigue's silent driver in India

## Cautions for This Goal
- **Giloy HILI warning:** Tinospora cordifolia linked to herb-induced liver injury — Kulkarni 2022 Hepatol Commun, Nagral 2021/2023. Limit to <3 months; monitor LFTs. Avoid in liver disease.
- Shilajit: avoid low-quality unprocessed resin (heavy metal contamination risk); buy ISS/AYUSH-certified
- KSM-66 + thyroid meds: monitor TSH
- Chyawanprash contains sugar — diabetics use sugar-free variant (sugar-free variant (varies by brand))
