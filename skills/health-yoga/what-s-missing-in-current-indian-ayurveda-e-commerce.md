# What's Missing in Current Indian Ayurveda E-Commerce

Eight structural features absent from Amazon, Nykaa, 1mg, and other platforms — and how Echo Shop fills each gap.

## 1. Grade Transparency
Amazon/Nykaa/1mg lists "Ashwagandha 60ct ₹260" with no extract type, no withanolide %, no origin. The consumer cannot differentiate raw churna from KSM-66. Price is the only available signal — and it correlates poorly with quality.

**Echo Shop:** Every Ashwagandha listing shows: extract type (KSM-66/Sensoril/raw), withanolide %, cultivation district, AYUSH mark tier.

## 2. Batch COAs Not Shown
AYUSH-licensed manufacturers perform heavy metals, microbial, and pesticide residue testing per Schedule T GMP requirements. These certificates of analysis (COAs) exist but are never surfaced to the end consumer on any major platform. The consumer has no way to verify the batch they received.

**Echo Shop:** Batch COA upload mandatory for all Category A herbs (Giloy, Shilajit, Ashwagandha, Triphala). QR code on product page → batch-specific lab report.

## 3. No Side-by-Side Comparison
1mg lists 30+ Ashwagandha brands. There is no comparison table for withanolide %, extract type, price-per-mg-active, AYUSH mark, or heavy metal status. The consumer must open 30 separate tabs.

**Echo Shop:** Comparison table native feature; filter by extract type, curcumin %, fulvic acid %, AYUSH tier.

## 4. Language Gap — English-Only
All major Indian ayurveda platforms are English-first. The classical Ayurvedic context (Sanskrit terms, classical usage, Charaka/Sushruta references) is lost even when present. Regional language browsing — Hindi, Marathi, Tamil — is absent.

**Echo Shop:** Hindi product names primary (Haldi, not Turmeric); Sanskrit classical term visible in product context; regional language support roadmapped.

## 5. Sourcing Opacity
"Organic Turmeric" on every platform. No mention of Pratapgarh Amla, Lakadong Haldi (Meghalaya, GI No.741), Nagori Ashwagandha (Nagaur, Rajasthan). GI-tagged origin is a legally registered quality signal that no marketplace makes searchable.

**Echo Shop:** GI origin badge; geographic filter (Pratapgarh / Lakadong / Nagori / Erode / Sangli); supplier farm location disclosure.

## 6. No Classical-Text References
Charaka Samhita, Sushruta Samhita, Bhavaprakasha Nighantu, Ashtanga Hridayam — these are the peer-reviewed evidence base for Ayurveda, 2,000+ years of clinical documentation. No Indian marketplace links classical dosage, usage context, or contraindications from these texts to their product pages.

**Echo Shop:** Each product page shows primary classical reference (e.g., Ashwagandha: Charaka *Chikitsa* 1.3.30; Triphala: *Chikitsa* 1.1.76).

## 7. Safety Information Underplayed
Giloy: HILI signal (Kulkarni 2022, Nagral 2021/2023) not visible on any major platform listing. Neem oil hepatotoxicity (documented in infants and at high doses in adults) not flagged. Ashwagandha thyroid interaction (ushna potency, potential T3/T4 alteration) not mentioned. Side-effect sections, where present, list "no known side effects."

**Echo Shop:** Mandatory safety ribbon on all Category A herbs. Contraindications (pregnancy, thyroid, liver conditions) shown before Add-to-Cart. Giloy HILI warning box on all Giloy listings.

## 8. No Goal-Based Discovery
Platforms are organised by brand, not goal. Consumer searching for "joint pain" gets a brand filter, not a goal filter. "Arthritis" and "joint health" lead to undifferentiated results across Himalaya, Dabur, Baidyanath — no differentiation by mechanism (anti-inflammatory vs collagen support vs vata pacification).

**Echo Shop:** 15 primary health goals (stress, sleep, joints, immunity, digestion, PCOS, skin, hair, energy, cognitive, diabetes, men, women, travel, seasonal). Product pages show primary goal + secondary goals. Browse by goal is the primary navigation path.
