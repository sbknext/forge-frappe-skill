Prepare for your 1:1 with your manager in 10 minutes to make the conversation useful for both sides.

## When to use
15–30 minutes before your regular 1:1 meeting.

## Mindset
The 1:1 is your meeting, not your manager's. Bring the agenda. Managers who get a blank stare when they ask "what do you want to talk about?" fill the time with status updates — waste for both parties.

## Template
```markdown
## 1:1 Prep — [Date]

### Updates (2 min max — not the point of the meeting)
- What I shipped / moved this week in one sentence
- One flag: anything my manager should know before the week ends?

### Discussion topics (10–20 min — this is the meeting)
Prioritise one of: career development, a hard problem, feedback I need, something I'm uncertain about.

1. [Main topic — what's the question or decision?]
2. [Secondary topic if time]

### Feedback I want to give my manager
(Managers rarely get feedback — use this slot)
- [Something working well]
- [Something I'd like done differently]

### Asks / needs
- [Resource / access / decision I need from my manager]

### Follow-up from last 1:1
- [Was last session's action item completed? Status:]
```

## Tips
- The best 1:1 topics: career trajectory, unblocking a decision, something you're uncertain about, direct feedback.
- Bring the doc. Share it with your manager 30 min before if they appreciate context; hold it for live discussion if they prefer spontaneous.
- If the conversation drifts to status updates, gently redirect: "I have a few things I'd like your input on — can we get to those?"
