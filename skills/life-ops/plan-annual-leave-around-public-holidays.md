Stack PTO against long weekends and festival clusters to maximise time off per day spent.

## When to use
Before the year begins — ideally December or first week of January — to book high-demand slots early.

## Steps
1. Download the official public holiday list for your state/country for the year.
2. Open a calendar view for the full year (Google Calendar, Notion, or paper).
3. Mark every public holiday. Circle ones that fall on Tuesday or Thursday — these create "bridge" opportunities.
4. For each bridge, calculate: leave days needed vs. total days off gained. Aim for ≥2:1 ratio.
5. Identify festival clusters (Diwali week, Christmas-New Year, Eid) where 3-4 days off yields 8-10 days total.
6. Block your top 5 windows in your leave management system immediately — before colleagues grab the same slots.
7. Leave 5 buffer days unplanned for emergencies, illness, or impromptu trips.

## Template
```
Year: ____

| Window | PH days | Leave days | Total off | Ratio | Status |
|--------|---------|------------|-----------|-------|--------|
| Diwali (Oct 28–Nov 3) | 2 | 3 | 9 | 3:1 | BOOKED |
| New Year (Dec 30–Jan 2) | 2 | 2 | 7 | 3.5:1 | BOOKED |
| Republic Day long weekend | 1 | 2 | 4 | 2:1 | PLANNED |
| Holi (March) | 1 | 1 | 3 | 3:1 | TBD |

Buffer days remaining: 5
```

## Tips
- India context: Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Delhi have different PH lists — download the state-specific one.
- Always check school calendars if travelling with kids — peak season pricing spikes 40-80%.
- Book flights/trains the day you confirm a window; delay = price jump.
