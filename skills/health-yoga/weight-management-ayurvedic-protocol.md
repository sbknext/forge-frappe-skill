# Weight Management — Ayurvedic Protocol

**Goal slug:** weight
**Indian-context relevance:** India has 70M+ obese adults; kapha-type obesity is endemic with sedentary urban diet — Ayurveda's lekhana (scraping) therapy complements calorie moderation.

## Primary Ingredients
- **Triphala** — metabolism enhancer + colon cleanse; 1 tsp warm water bedtime
- **Guggul** (Commiphora mukul, purified) — lipid-lowering + thyroid-stimulating lekhana; 500mg BID
- **Haldi + Piperine** — curcumin activates PPAR-γ pathway (fat cell differentiation); 500mg + 5mg

## Supporting Ingredients
- **Tulsi** — insulin sensitiser; 2 caps or tea
- **Giloy** (short-term) — metabolic immunomodulator
- **Amla** — fat metabolism via vitamin C; antioxidant
- **Vrikshamla** (Garcinia cambogioides) — HCA hydroxycitric acid; 500mg 30 min pre-meal

## Daily Regimen
- **Morning (empty stomach):** Warm water + lemon (classical ushna jala protocol) + Amla juice 20ml
- **Pre-meal (30 min before):** Vrikshamla 500mg
- **With meals:** Haldi-piperine 500mg
- **Post-meal:** Guggul 500mg BID with warm water (not ghee)
- **Bedtime:** Triphala 1 tsp warm water

## Timeline & Expectations
- **Week 4:** Bloating reduces; energy on lower calories improves
- **Week 8–12:** 0.5–1 kg/week loss achievable with calorie deficit
- Herbs enhance effect; without diet compliance, results minimal

## Lifestyle Anchors (Charaka non-negotiables)
- Calorie moderation (500 kcal deficit/day) — no herb overrides thermodynamics
- Kapha-pacifying diet: warm, light, spiced food; avoid dairy excess and sweets
- Daily exercise minimum 45 min — activates agni (metabolic fire)

## Cautions for This Goal
- **Giloy HILI warning:** Tinospora cordifolia linked to herb-induced liver injury (HILI) — Kulkarni 2022 Hepatol Commun, Nagral 2021/2023. Limit to <3 months; monitor LFTs.
- Guggul: thyroid interactions — monitor TSH if on levothyroxine; can enhance thyroid activity
- Vrikshamla (Garcinia): avoid in liver disease; discontinue if GI pain occurs
- Guggul has mild anticoagulant effect; inform physician if on blood thinners
