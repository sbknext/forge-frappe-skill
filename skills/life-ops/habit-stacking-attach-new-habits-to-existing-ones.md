Anchor new behaviours to existing daily triggers so they require near-zero willpower.

## When to use
Whenever adding a new habit. Works for physical, mental, or digital habits.

## Core concept
Habit stacking formula: **After/Before [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT].**

Your existing habits are reliable triggers. Piggybacking removes the activation energy problem.

## Steps
1. List your current reliable habits (things you do >5 days/week without thinking).
2. For each new habit you want, identify the best anchor from your list.
3. Write the full stack statement. Be specific: time, place, action.
4. Run it for 14 days minimum. Track a simple ✓/✗ in a notebook or app.
5. After 14 days, review — did the stack hold? If not, try a different anchor.

## Template
```
Anchor habits I already have:
[ ] Morning coffee
[ ] Brushing teeth (PM)
[ ] Sitting at desk after lunch
[ ] Closing laptop for the day

My habit stacks:
| After/Before | Current habit | New habit | Days tracked |
|---|---|---|---|
| After | morning coffee | 10 min reading | 0/14 |
| Before | brushing teeth (PM) | 5 min journal | 0/14 |
| After | sitting at desk (lunch) | 2 min planning next task | 0/14 |
```

## Tips
- Keep new habits under 5 minutes initially. Expansion comes naturally.
- "Before" stacks (pre-anchors) work well for habits you keep skipping at end-of-day.
- Don't stack more than 2 new habits simultaneously — one always gets dropped.
