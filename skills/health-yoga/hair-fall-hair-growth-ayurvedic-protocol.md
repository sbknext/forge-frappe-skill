# Hair Fall & Hair Growth — Ayurvedic Protocol

**Goal slug:** hair-fall
**Indian-context relevance:** Hard water, iron deficiency, stress, and hormonal PCOS make hair fall India's most common cosmetic complaint — Ayurveda addresses all four root causes.

## Primary Ingredients
- **Amla** (Emblica officinalis) — DHT inhibitor + scalp antioxidant; 20ml juice/day oral; topical in oil
- **Bhringaraj** (Eclipta alba) — kesharaja (king of hair); bhringaraj-amla oil weekly scalp massage
- **Brahmi taila** — stress-driven hair fall (pitta-vata); 300mg Brahmi cap for chronic stress root cause

## Supporting Ingredients
- **Triphala** — gut health (B-vitamin absorption); 1 tsp bedtime
- **Shatavari** — hormonal hair fall in women (PCOS/post-partum); 500mg BID
- **Neem** — dandruff-induced hair fall; 1 cap AM

## Daily Regimen
- **Morning (empty stomach):** Amla juice 20ml; Brahmi 300mg cap
- **Post-breakfast:** Shatavari 500mg (women only)
- **Bedtime:** Triphala 1 tsp warm water
- **Weekly (ideally Sunday night):** Warm bhringaraj-amla oil massage, leave overnight, wash AM

## Timeline & Expectations
- **Week 4:** Scalp oiliness/dandruff reduces; shedding may peak (telogen phase purge)
- **Week 8:** Shedding visibly reduces
- **Week 12:** New growth visible at hairline
- **Week 16:** Density improvement — one full hair cycle (anagen)

## Lifestyle Anchors (Charaka non-negotiables)
- Iron-rich diet (spinach, sesame, dates) — deficiency is #1 hair-fall driver in Indian women
- Avoid tight hairstyles and heat tools during active fall protocol
- Consistent sleep schedule — cortisol disruption = pitta aggravation = hair miniaturisation

## Cautions for This Goal
- Rule out thyroid and ferritin deficiency first; Ayurveda works alongside, not instead of, correction
- Shatavari contraindicated in oestrogen-sensitive conditions (fibroid, ER+ cancer)
- Brahmi (Bacopa monnieri) — NOT Centella asiatica; verify product species
