# AYUSH Certification Guide for Indian Buyers

Understanding the certification hierarchy separates ₹95 raw churna from ₹599 clinically-tested extracts — often with the same product name on both.

## The Core Problem
"Ashwagandha 60 capsules" on Amazon at ₹260 and on Echo Shop at ₹599 can both legally say "AYUSH-certified." One holds AYUSH Standard (Schedule T Indian GMP); one may hold AYUSH Premium (WHO-GMP). The consumer has no way to distinguish from the listing.

## AYUSH Standard Mark
- **Standard:** Schedule T of Drugs & Cosmetics Act — Indian GMP baseline
- **Scope:** Manufacturing premises, hygiene, basic quality control
- **Contaminant limits:** Indian Pharmacopoeia standards
- **Products:** Domestic retail market

## AYUSH Premium Mark
- **Standard:** WHO-GMP (stricter than Schedule T)
- **Scope:** International-grade manufacturing, documentation, traceability
- **Contaminant limits:** Stricter — aligned with EU/US limits for heavy metals, pesticide residue
- **Usage:** Primarily export products; some D2C Indian brands now voluntarily acquire it for domestic premium positioning
- **Registry:** ~146 Premium / ~197 Standard registered (non-government estimate as of 2024; verify current numbers at QCI/NABCB official registry before citing)

## NPOP (National Programme for Organic Production)
- **Issued by:** APEDA (Agricultural and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority)
- **Coverage:** Agricultural inputs (farm-level organic certification)
- **Equivalence:** EU Organic (Regulation 834/2007) and USDA NOP bilateral equivalence agreements
- **What it means:** No synthetic pesticides, GMO, ionising radiation in cultivation
- **What it doesn't cover:** Manufacturing GMP, heavy metal testing post-harvest

## FSSAI Nutraceutical Regulations
- Ingestible Ayurvedic products marketed with health claims fall under FSSAI nutraceutical category (Food Safety and Standards Act 2006)
- FSSAI License Number must appear on all edible supplement labels
- Different from AYUSH license (AYUSH = drug claims; FSSAI = food/supplement claims)
- Jurisdiction overlap: some ashwagandha capsules carry both; some only FSSAI (no drug claim); AYUSH-licensed products cannot simultaneously make food-product-only claims

## GI Tags (Geographic Indication)
| Product | GI Number | Year | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lakadong Turmeric (Meghalaya) | GI No.741 | March 2024 | 6.8–7.5% curcumin defensible; highest Indian GI haldi |
| Erode Turmeric (Tamil Nadu) | Earlier registration | 2019 | High curcumin volume producer |
| Sangli Turmeric (Maharashtra) | Earlier registration | 2018 | Major commercial haldi belt |
| Waigaon Turmeric (Maharashtra) | Separate GI | — | Vidarbha origin |

GI is a legal origin protection, not a quality standard per se — but it sets minimum regional origin traceability requirements.

## NABL-Tested Lab Reports
- **NABL:** National Accreditation Board for Testing and Calibration Laboratories (Department of Science & Technology, GoI)
- **For buyers, this matters:** A NABL-accredited lab's COA is legally defensible; non-NABL lab results are not
- **Per-batch testing minimum:** Heavy metals (Pb, As, Hg, Cd), microbial (total plate count, E.coli, Salmonella, Aflatoxin), pesticide residue (organochlorine/organophosphate), and for standardised extracts — active compound % (curcumin, withanolides, fulvic acid)

## USFDA Approved Facility Claims
- Some Indian D2C Ayurveda brands claim "manufactured in USFDA-approved facility" — e.g., Kapiva
- This means: the manufacturing plant has passed a USFDA inspection and is registered; it does NOT mean the specific Ayurvedic product is FDA-approved (no Ayurvedic products have USFDA drug approval)
- Useful as a proxy for WHO-GMP level manufacturing discipline

## Echo Shop Certification Tiers (Filterable Badges)
1. **Premium** — AYUSH Premium Mark (WHO-GMP) + NABL batch COA + GI origin
2. **Standard** — AYUSH Standard Mark + FSSAI + batch COA
3. **Basic** — FSSAI only
4. **GI Origin** — Geographic Indication badge (separate filter)
5. **Organic** — NPOP certified (separate filter)

Same product name, vastly different quality assurance. Echo Shop makes this visible before Add-to-Cart.
