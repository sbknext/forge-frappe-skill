# Winter Ritual: Vata-Warming Rasayana Protocol

**Anchor:** October–February (Hemanta/Shishira), or when cold, dry, joint stiffness increases
**Goal:** Agni protection, ojas storage, vata pacification for the cold season

## Sequence (Morning)
1. **Chyawanprash 1–2 tsp** — the classical winter Rasayana; Amla-based, 35–40 herbs; take before warm milk
2. **Ashwagandha-milk** — 300 mg KSM-66 in 200 ml warm full-fat milk + pinch Trikatu
3. **Shilajit 250 mg** — in warm milk post-breakfast (peak season for use; ushna suits winter)
4. **Abhyanga (oil self-massage)** — sesame oil, 10–15 min daily or 3x/week before bath; especially joints
5. **Haldi-doodh at night** — 200 ml warm milk, ¼ tsp Lakadong turmeric, black pepper, ghee

## Why (Ayurvedic Rationale)
Classical Ayurveda considers Hemanta (early winter) the best season for Rasayana (rejuvenation) therapy — agni is naturally strongest, body retains nutrients best. Ashtanga Hridayam *Ritucharya*: "In winter, one should use unctuous, sour, salty foods; sesame paste, ghee, milk." Chyawanprash is the most ancient documented Rasayana formula (Charaka *Chikitsa Sthana* 1.62) — Amla prevents Vit C degradation in churna formula via tannin bonding. Shilajit at its full ushna dose is classical winter medicine; contrast with summer where it should be halved.

## Cautions
- Chyawanprash contains sugar — sugar-free variant (varies by brand) for diabetics
- Full abhyanga: avoid during active fever, skin infections, immediately post-meal
- Shilajit: full winter dose; reduce in summer (see Summer Ritual)
- Trikatu in Ashwagandha-milk: skip if Pitta symptoms present even in winter
