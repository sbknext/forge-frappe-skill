# Working Professional Ritual: Screen, Stress, Sedentary Urban Indian

**Anchor:** Daily; morning protocol + evening protocol; urban India context
**Goal:** Counter screen-induced Pitta-Vata aggravation, cortisol creep, sedentary kapha accumulation

## Morning Stack (before 9 AM)
1. **Ashwagandha KSM-66 300 mg** — cortisol management; take with breakfast, not empty stomach
2. **Brahmi (Bacopa monnieri) 300 mg** — cognitive performance; synergistic with Ashwagandha for HPA axis
3. **Amla juice 20–30 ml** — screen-induced oxidative stress; highest natural Vit C source
4. **Tulsi tea 1 cup** — adaptogenic baseline; reduces morning cortisol spike

## Evening Stack (6–7 PM or post-work)
5. **Triphala 1 tsp** in warm water at 9–10 PM — bowel regulation (sedentary gut); classical 1:1:1 ratio
6. **Triphala eye wash weekly** — dissolve ¼ tsp in cooled boiled water; wash eyes; classical netra tarpana for digital eye strain

## Gender-Specific Add-ons
- **Women**: Shatavari 1 tsp in warm milk at night — hormonal balance, PCOS prevention, Pitta-prone reproductive tissue
- **Men**: Shilajit 250 mg morning with milk — testosterone support, mitochondrial energy (sedentary men have higher oxidative load)

## Why (Ayurvedic Rationale)
Urban professional dosha analysis: 8+ hours screen = Pitta aggravation (eyes are Pitta organ); sedentary = Kapha accumulation (metabolism slows); irregular meals + deadlines = Vata aggravation (nervous system). The stack addresses all three simultaneously. Brahmi (*Bacopa monnieri*) and Ashwagandha are the two most validated Rasayana for mental performance — classical medhya duo. Triphala is the only tridoshic bowel regulator suitable for long-term use.

## Cautions
- Brahmi (*Bacopa monnieri*): slows information processing slightly during first 4–6 weeks of use; effects build over 8–12 weeks; do not expect immediate cognitive boost
- Ashwagandha + Brahmi morning: may cause mild sedation in some; if so, shift Brahmi to evening
- Triphala: regular use fine long-term; increase water intake to 2.5+ L/day
- Shatavari: see summer ritual for hormone-sensitive tumour caution
- Shilajit: skip during fever, infections; see earlier caution on fulvic acid % authentication
