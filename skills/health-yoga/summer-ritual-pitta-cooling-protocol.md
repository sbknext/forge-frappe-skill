# Summer Ritual: Pitta-Cooling Protocol

**Anchor:** April–July (Grishma/Varsha onset), or whenever body heat / skin inflammation increases
**Goal:** Pitta-aggravation prevention — cooling, hydration, sheetala (cooling) herbs

## Sequence (Morning)
1. **Amla juice 30 ml** — cold or room temperature (NOT warm); Pitta-pacifying, highest natural Vit C
2. **Rose-petal sherbet** — gulkand (rose petal preserve) 1 tsp in cold milk or rose water; cooling Pitta antidote
3. **Shatavari milk** — 1 tsp Shatavari powder in cool milk (medhya + cooling for women; also men on hot days)
4. **Brahmi (Bacopa monnieri) 300 mg** — cooling medhya; reduces mental Pitta (irritability, sharp focus fatigue)
5. **Vetiver water (khus)** — vetiver-infused water as summer drinking water; cooling and diuretic

## Adjustments (REDUCE these in summer — all ushna/heating)
- **Haldi**: reduce to pinch (not standard ¼ tsp dose)
- **Shilajit**: halve dose or skip on peak summer days (very ushna)
- **Ashwagandha**: reduce to 150 mg or skip; replace with Shatavari
- **Trikatu/Hingvashtak**: avoid unless digestion severely impaired

## Why (Ayurvedic Rationale)
Ashtanga Hridayam *Ritucharya* chapter: Grishma (summer) = Pitta accumulation; Varsha (monsoon) = Pitta provocation. Ushna (hot potency) herbs aggravate Pitta → skin rashes, acidity, anger, bleeding disorders. Sheetala (cooling) protocol: amla, shatavari, brahmi, rose, vetiver are all shita virya (cold potency) per Nighantu.

## Cautions
- Shatavari estrogenic — caution in ER+ hormone-sensitive tumour history; consult oncologist
- Gulkand high sugar — diabetics use minimally or skip
- Do NOT take vetiver internally as extract/oil — only water infusion is safe
- Cooling herbs may dampen agni if overdone — maintain some ginger in pre-meal ritual
