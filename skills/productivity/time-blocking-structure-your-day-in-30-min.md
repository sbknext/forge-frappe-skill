Block your calendar the night before to protect deep work and stop reactive days.

## When to use
Every evening before the next workday (5 minutes). Or Sunday evening for the full week (30 minutes).

## Principles
- **Protect one deep-work block daily** — uninterrupted 90-120 min for your hardest task.
- **Batch shallow work** — meetings, email, Slack in defined windows, not sprinkled throughout.
- **Show travel time between meetings** — even virtual ones need 5 min transition.

## Daily template
```
[BEFORE WORK]
6:30–8:00   — Morning routine

[DEEP WORK — protect this first]
9:00–11:00  — [Single focus task: most important deliverable today]

[SHALLOW BATCH 1]
11:00–12:00 — Email + Slack triage

[LUNCH / RECOVERY]
12:00–1:00  — Lunch

[MEETINGS (batch here if possible)]
1:00–2:00   — [Meeting 1]
2:00–3:00   — [Meeting 2]
3:00–3:15   — Transition / notes

[DEEP WORK 2 or collaborative work]
3:15–5:00   — [Second priority task or reviews/PRs]

[SHUTDOWN]
5:00–5:30   — Inbox zero + tomorrow's time blocks
```

## Tips
- Deep work block: phone on DND, close Slack, close email. 90 min of true focus beats 5 hours of fragmented effort.
- Over-scheduled days: when calendar is full, protect the deep-work block before accepting any more meetings.
- If you can't block your own calendar (too many mandatory meetings), block just 60 min daily — even that compounds.
