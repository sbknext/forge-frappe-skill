# Adulteration Field Guide for Indian Ayurveda Consumers

Per-ingredient detection guide. All tests are consumer-doable without lab equipment unless noted.

## Haldi (Turmeric)
**Common adulterants:** Lead chromate (artificial yellow), metanil yellow (coal-tar dye), chalk, starch.
- **Lead chromate test:** Dissolve ¼ tsp in 5 ml water; red/orange streaks = lead chromate. Genuine haldi = clear yellow-orange dissolution.
- **Metanil yellow test:** Add few drops HCl to solution; turns pink = metanil yellow. Genuine = no colour change with HCl.
- **Chalk test:** HCl addition causes effervescence (bubbles) = chalk added.
- **Starch test:** Add iodine drops to solution; turns blue/black = starch filler.
**Buy recommendation:** Whole Lakadong or Erode/Sangli rhizomes; grind at home. GI-origin powders with batch COA second choice.

## Ashwagandha
**Genuine markers:** Characteristic horse-urine smell (withanolide content indicator); pale grey-brown colour (not bleached white).
**Red flags:** No smell; bright white powder (bleached); no withanolide % on label.
**Verification:** Demand withanolide % COA from batch; request single-origin Nagaur source declaration; AYUSH Premium Mark.

## Tulsi
**Genuine markers:** Species name on label (*Ocimum tenuiflorum* = Krishna/Rama; *Ocimum gratissimum* = Vana/highest eugenol).
**Adulteration:** Other Ocimum species, especially *O. basilicum* (sweet basil) substituted in bulk.
**Buy recommendation:** NPOP organic certified; single-species named; FSSAI license visible.

## Triphala
**Common adulteration:** Cheap Bibhitaki fruit shells used as filler instead of whole dried fruit pulp; ratio deviated from 1:1:1.
**Verification:** Demand Amalaki:Bibhitaki:Haritaki ratio disclosure on label; genuine Triphala smells astringent-sour (Amalaki dominant), not just slightly astringent.

## Neem
**Genuine markers:** Green colour (not brown — brown = oxidised/old stock); bitter-aromatic smell.
**Verification:** AYUSH license; capsule brand with NABL-tested batch; avoid Neem oil taken internally (hepatotoxic in infants, documented cases in adults at high dose).

## Brahmi
**Most common mislabelling in India:** *Centella asiatica* (Mandukaparni) sold as "Brahmi" — different plant, different mechanism.
**Verification:** Latin name on label must be *Bacopa monnieri*. "Brahmi" alone is insufficient. If label says "Centella" or "Gotu Kola" — that is Mandukaparni, not classical Brahmi.

## Giloy (*Tinospora cordifolia*)
> **⚠ HILI CAUTION:** Giloy has been associated with herb-induced liver injury in peer-reviewed Indian case series (Kulkarni 2022 *Hepatol Commun*; Nagral 2021/2023 *J Clin Exp Hepatol*). Additionally, *Tinospora crispa* — a hepatotoxic look-alike species — is reportedly sold as Giloy in some markets.
**Verification:** Demand botanical authentication certificate (microscopy or DNA barcoding for bulk); reject products without species authentication. Use maximum 2 weeks continuously; LFT monitoring if longer.

## Shatavari
**Adulteration:** Cheaper *Asparagus* species (non-racemosus) substituted. Fresh-root sourcing claim without supply chain transparency = unverifiable.
**Verification:** Latin name *Asparagus racemosus*; AYUSH license; ideally NABL-tested for saponin content.

## Shilajit
**5 consumer authentication tests (no lab required):**
1. **Water test:** Drop in water → genuine dissolves completely, water turns clear golden-brown, no undissolved particles.
2. **Flame test:** Touch small piece to flame → genuine bubbles and forms ash, does NOT melt and drip like wax.
3. **Alcohol test:** Genuine does NOT dissolve in alcohol — forms rubber-like ball. Fake fulvic acid product dissolves.
4. **Cold test:** Genuine Shilajit hardens in refrigerator, softens at body temperature (pliable at 37°C).
5. **Taste test:** Intensely bitter-smoky-metallic-petrochemical. NEVER sweet. Sweet = adulterated.

**COA requirement:** Pb <10 ppm, As <3 ppm, Hg <1 ppm, Cd <0.3 ppm (WHO limits). Fulvic acid 15–60%; >70% = synthetic fulvic acid added = adulterated.

## Amla
**Juice adulteration:** Citric acid + colorants + water sold as "Amla juice." Genuine: no preservatives, cold-pressed, measurable natural Vit C (Pratapgarh-origin > 600 mg/100 ml).
**Powder adulteration:** Wood pulp, inferior species.
**Verification:** Cold-pressed label; third-party Vit C lab test per batch; Pratapgarh UP origin; FSSAI registration.
