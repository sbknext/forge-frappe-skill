# Shatavari — *Asparagus racemosus*

**Sanskrit/Regional:** Shatavari ("she who possesses 100 husbands"), Shatamuli, Bahusuta; Satavari (Hindi), Shimai-shadavari (Tamil), Pilligadalu (Telugu)
**Active compounds:** Shatavarins I–X (steroidal saponins), sitosterol, asparagamine A, racemosol, phytoestrogens, mucilage

> Queen of Herbs for women — foremost Stanyajanana (galactagogue) and female Rasayana per Charaka

## Forms & Indian Pricing (2026)
- Patanjali Shatavar Churna 100g ₹200
- Patanjali Shatavaryadi Churna 60g ₹75
- Himalaya root extract 250mg 60ct ₹300 (sale ₹240–275)
- Shatavari kalpa (milk-soluble granules): classical postpartum preparation
- Shatavari ghrita: ghee-based for Vata-Pitta conditions

## Dosage
- **Powder:** 3–5g twice daily (BID) with warm milk + ghee
- **Capsule/extract:** 500mg × 2/day
- **Kalpa granules:** 1–2 tsp in warm milk (postpartum)
- Sustained use 3–6 months for hormonal conditions

## Ayurvedic Properties
- **Rasa:** Madhura (sweet), Tikta (bitter)
- **Guna:** Guru (heavy), Snigdha (unctuous)
- **Virya:** Sheeta (cool)
- **Vipaka:** Madhura
- **Dosha:** Vata-Pitta shamak; mild Kapha-aggravating
- **Karma:** Stanyajanana (lactation promoting), Balya (strength), Rasayana, Vrishya (aphrodisiac), Medhya, Shukral (reproductive tonic)
- **Classical refs:** Charaka Sutrasthana balya gana; Bhavaprakasha Shatpushpadi varga

## Health Benefits
- **Lactation (galactagogue):** RCT 2025 *J Obstet Gynaecol* (PMID 41055223) — Asparagus racemosus root extract 300mg × 72h postpartum showed significantly improved milk volume
- **Hormonal balance:** PCOS, PMS, perimenopausal hot flashes/dryness/libido — shatavarins phytoestrogenic activity
- **Female fertility:** luteal phase support, follicular development animal studies
- **Gastric ulcer/hyperacidity:** mucilaginous demulcent effect — coats gastric mucosa; anti-H.pylori preclinical
- **Adaptogen:** HPA axis modulation; cortisol buffering
- **Antioxidant:** high flavonoid + saponin content
- **Antitussive:** classical use for dry cough (Sheeta virya + unctuous)
- **Immunity:** racemosol immunomodulation

## Common Preparations
- **Shatavari kalpa:** granules + warm milk + ghee — classical postpartum Rasayana
- **Shatavari ghrita:** ghee preparation for Vata-Pitta female conditions
- **Women's vitality stack:** Shatavari + Ashwagandha + Vidari Kanda
- **Classical formulas:** Phalghrita, Shatpushpadi ghrita (Bhavaprakasha), Narayana taila, Shatavaryadi kwath

## Sourcing
Sub-Himalayan foothills, Central India, Western Ghats; mix of wild-harvested and cultivated (MP/Maharashtra). Wild supply declining — cultivated preferred for sustainability.

## Best Paired With
- **Women's vitality:** Ashwagandha (adaptogen + strength)
- **Lactation:** Yashtimadhu (Mulethi) — soothing + galactagogue synergy
- **Ulcer/acidity:** Yashtimadhu + Shatavari
- **Reproductive:** Vidari Kanda (Pueraria tuberosa)
- **Classical formulas:** Phalghrita, Shatavari kalpa, Shatpushpadi ghrita, Narayana taila

## Contraindications
- **Estrogen-sensitive cancers (breast/uterine/ovarian):** phytoestrogen content — consult oncologist
- **Hormonal contraceptives:** theoretical additive estrogenic effect — monitor
- **Hypoglycemics:** additive blood sugar lowering
- **Diuretics:** additive diuretic effect from saponins
- **Kapha-dominant constitution:** Guru+Snigdha qualities may worsen congestion, weight, lethargy
- **Excess edema:** Snigdha (unctuous) may aggravate

## Adulteration Markers & Certifications
- Cheaper Asparagus species (A. sarmentosus, A. adscendens) substituted
- Look for: COA with shatavarin I saponin standardization %, fresh-root sourcing, GMP, AYUSH Standard Mark
