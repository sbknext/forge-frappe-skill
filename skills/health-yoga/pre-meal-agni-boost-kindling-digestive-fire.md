# Pre-Meal Agni Boost: Kindling Digestive Fire

**Anchor:** 5–10 min before main meals (lunch/dinner)
**Goal:** Deepana — kindling jatharagni (gastric fire) before food intake

## Sequence
1. **Ginger slice + rock salt + lemon** — 1 thin slice raw ginger, pinch saindhava lavana (rock salt), few drops lemon; chew slowly 5 min before eating
2. **Hingvashtak churna ¼ tsp** — in warm water or ghee (alternative to ginger for severe low-agni)
3. **Trikatu pinch** — blend of dry ginger (shunti) + black pepper (maricha) + long pepper (pippali); ¼ tsp in warm water if digestion very sluggish

Use ONE approach, not all three simultaneously.

## Why (Ayurvedic Rationale)
Charaka Samhita (*Chikitsa Sthana* 15.70) prescribes deepaniya (digestive stimulants) before meals to prevent ama formation. Gingerols stimulate bile and digestive enzyme secretion. Rock salt (saindhava) is the only salt Ayurveda considers medicinal — sodium chloride with 84 trace minerals. Hing (asafoetida) in Hingvashtak is a potent carminative; long pepper in Trikatu has bioavailability-enhancing properties analogous to piperine.

## Cautions
- Avoid in Pitta-excess (acid reflux, GERD, hyperacidity) — ushna herbs will aggravate
- Hingvashtak contains hing (asafoetida) — avoid in pregnancy
- Trikatu pippali (long pepper): do not use continuously >30 days; classical texts recommend cycling
- Not required if agni is already strong (healthy appetite, no gas/bloating)
