A 15-minute weekly check-in to track OKR progress and surface blockers before they kill KRs.

## When to use
Every Monday morning (or end of Friday). Takes 15 minutes when the template is pre-filled.

## Steps
1. For each KR, update the current metric from source of truth (dashboard, spreadsheet, Jira).
2. Calculate the trend: on-track, at-risk, or off-track.
3. For anything at-risk or off-track, write ONE next action.
4. Share the check-in with your manager or team if OKRs are shared.

## Template
```markdown
## OKR Check-in — Week [N] ([Date])

### O1: [Objective title]

| KR | Target | Current | Trend | Next action |
|----|--------|---------|-------|-------------|
| KR1.1 | 100 users | 67 users | ON-TRACK | — |
| KR1.2 | 90% uptime | 97.2% | ON-TRACK | — |

### O2: [Objective title]

| KR | Target | Current | Trend | Next action |
|----|--------|---------|-------|-------------|
| KR2.1 | 5 deals closed | 2 | AT-RISK | Follow up with 3 warm leads this week |

---

**This week's OKR focus**: [One sentence — where are you spending deliberate effort?]

**Blockers requiring help**:
- [Blocker] — need [specific help] from [person]
```

## Tips
- Trend definitions: ON-TRACK = will hit target at current pace; AT-RISK = possible with effort; OFF-TRACK = needs plan change.
- Weekly check-in should not take >15 minutes. If it does, the KR metrics are too hard to find — fix the source of truth.
- Don't update OKRs mid-quarter to make them easier. Update the action, not the target.
