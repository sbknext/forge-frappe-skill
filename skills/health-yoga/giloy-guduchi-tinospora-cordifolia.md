# Giloy (Guduchi) — *Tinospora cordifolia*

**Sanskrit/Regional:** Guduchi, Amrita ("nectar of immortality"), Madhuparni, Chinnaruha; Gulvel (Marathi), Seenthil (Tamil), Tippa-teega (Telugu)
**Active compounds:** Tinosporin, berberine, cordifoliosides A-E, palmatine, magnoflorine, clerodane furano-diterpenoids, polysaccharides

---

> ⚠️ **HILI Caution — Herb-Induced Liver Injury:**
> Multiple peer-reviewed case series document acute hepatitis with autoimmune features in Giloy users:
> - Nagral A et al. *J Clin Exp Hepatol* 2021 (case series)
> - Kulkarni AV et al. *Hepatol Commun* 2022 (multicenter India series — 50+ cases)
> - LiverTox (NIH) 2024 entry: Tinospora cordifolia listed as hepatotoxic herb
> Surge in self-medication during COVID-19 (2020–2022) correlated with case increase.
> **Mechanism:** possibly clerodane furano-diterpenoids; also linked to *Tinospora crispa* species confusion.
> **Avoid if:** pre-existing liver disease, autoimmune hepatitis, women >55 with planned chronic use.
> **Limit:** <3 months continuous use; monitor LFTs if using >6 weeks.

## Contraindications
- **Pre-existing liver disease / autoimmune hepatitis:** absolute caution (see HILI box above)
- **Autoimmune conditions (RA/lupus/MS):** immunostimulation may trigger flares
- **Pregnancy:** avoid
- **Diabetes medications:** additive hypoglycemia — monitor blood sugar
- **Chronic use >3 months:** monitor LFTs; stop and consult physician if symptoms appear
- **Species confusion:** *Tinospora crispa* often adulterated in — more hepatotoxic alkaloids; demand authenticated T. cordifolia

## Adulteration Markers & Certifications
