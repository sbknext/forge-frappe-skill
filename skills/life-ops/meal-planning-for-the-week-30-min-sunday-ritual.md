Plan weekday meals in 30 minutes on Sunday to reduce decision fatigue and grocery waste.

## When to use
Every Sunday before grocery shopping. Invest 30 minutes to save 30+ minutes of daily "what's for dinner" decisions.

## Steps
1. Check what's already in your fridge, pantry, and freezer.
2. Plan 5 weekday dinners (or fewer if you eat out regularly). Lunch can repeat.
3. Write a grocery list from the meal plan gaps.
4. Keep 1-2 fallback meals for when plans change (eggs + bread, khichdi, pasta).
5. Prep anything shareable on Sunday: chop vegetables, cook a base grain, marinate protein.

## Template
```
Week of ____

| Day | Dinner | Lunch (if cooking) | Prep needed |
|-----|--------|--------------------|-------------|
| Mon | Dal makhani + rice | Leftover | Soak dal tonight |
| Tue | Pasta aglio olio | Curd rice | — |
| Wed | Paneer bhurji + roti | — (eat out) | — |
| Thu | Soup + toast | Leftover | — |
| Fri | Takeout / eat out | — | — |

Fallback meals available:
1. Eggs + bread (always stocked)
2. Instant noodles

Grocery list:
- [ ] Paneer (200g)
- [ ] Pasta
- [ ] Tomatoes
- ...
```

## Tips
- Plan 3-4 meals, not 7. Planned meals cover the high-stress weekdays; weekends are flexible.
- Batch cooking 2x the recipe on Monday saves a full cooking session on Wednesday.
- India context: Big Basket / Blinkit orders placed Sunday morning arrive before evening — do your shop right after writing the list.
