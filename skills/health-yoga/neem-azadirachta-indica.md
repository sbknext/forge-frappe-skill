# Neem — *Azadirachta indica*

**Sanskrit/Regional:** Nimba, Arishta ("reliever of sickness"), Sarba-roga-nibarini ("curer of all diseases"); Vembu (Tamil), Vepa (Telugu), Kadulimb (Marathi)
**Active compounds:** Azadirachtin, nimbin, nimbidin, nimbolide, gedunin, quercetin, limonoids; all plant parts used

## Forms & Indian Pricing (2026)
- Himalaya Neem 60ct ₹260 (sale ₹221–240)

- Neem oil 100ml ₹150–350 (**topical only**)
- Powder 100g ₹80–200
- Fresh leaves: free from household/community trees

## Dosage
- **Capsules:** 250–500mg × 2/day
- **Leaf powder:** 1–3g/day
- **Neem oil:** topical only — **never oral; toxic internally**

## Ayurvedic Properties
- **Rasa:** Tikta (bitter), Kashaya (astringent)
- **Guna:** Laghu (light), Ruksha (dry)
- **Virya:** **Sheeta (cold)** — rare among bitters; most bitter herbs are Ushna
- **Vipaka:** Katu
- **Dosha:** Kapha-Pitta shamak; **Vata-aggravating** (Ruksha+Sheeta)
- **Karma:** Krimighna (antiparasitic), Kushtaghna (skin), Jwaraghna (antipyretic), Raktashodhaka (blood purifier)
- **Classical refs:** Charaka Kushtaghna mahakashaya; Sushruta Samhita skin disorder chapter

## Health Benefits
- **Skin:** acne, eczema, psoriasis — oral + topical; nimbidin anti-inflammatory
- **Anti-diabetic:** azadirachtin + nimbidiol — Indian J Exp Biol studies; adjunct only
- **Dental:** anti-plaque datun (neem twig); comparable to chlorhexidine in some trials
- **Wound healing:** antimicrobial + astringent topical
- **Anti-parasitic:** classical Krimighna — intestinal worms
- **Hair/scalp:** anti-dandruff shampoo; neem-coconut oil scalp application
- **Liver protection:** vs paracetamol-induced damage (Indian J Physiol Pharmacol 2000)
- **Antimalarial:** classical use + gedunin mechanism
- **Blood purification:** Raktashodhaka classical role for chronic skin + fever conditions

## Common Preparations
- **Skin:** neem-tulsi kadha (internal), neem-haldi face paste (topical)
- **Scalp:** neem leaves boiled in coconut oil
- **Dental:** neem datun (twig) or neem oil diluted mouthwash
- **Chickenpox tradition:** neem leaves in bathwater (antimicrobial/antipruritic)
- **Classical formulas:** Panchanimba churna, Nimbadi kashayam, Mahatikta ghrita

## Sourcing
Pan-Indian; Rajasthan/MP/Karnataka for commercial cultivation. Practically every village has neem trees — fresh leaves are the most accessible form.

## Best Paired With
- **Skin:** Haldi (anti-inflammatory synergy)
- **Blood:** Manjistha (Rubia cordifolia)
- **Fever/immunity:** Giloy + Neem combination
- **Immunity base:** Amla + Neem
- **Classical formulas:** Panchanimba churna, Nimbadi kashayam, Mahatikta ghrita (skin-metabolic)

## Contraindications
⚠️ **Neem oil hepatotoxicity:** Oral neem oil → Reye-like syndrome in infants/toddlers (Sinniah 1989, multiple pediatric case reports). **NEVER give neem oil orally to children.**
- **Pregnancy:** abortifacient properties; spermicidal — avoid in conception attempts
- **Male fertility:** sperm count reduction (high dose animal + some human data)
- **Hypoglycemia risk:** additive with antidiabetics; monitor blood sugar
- **Autoimmune conditions:** immunomodulatory effects may be unpredictable
- **Long-term high dose:** potential hepatotoxicity; cycle use
- **Vata constitution/Vata diseases:** Sheeta+Ruksha qualities aggravate

## Adulteration Markers & Certifications
- Mixed with cheaper bitter leaves (chirata, kalmegh) in powder form
- Genuine: green color (not brown/khaki), intensely bitter-aromatic smell
- Look for: AYUSH licensed manufacturer, GMP, species name *Azadirachta indica* on label
