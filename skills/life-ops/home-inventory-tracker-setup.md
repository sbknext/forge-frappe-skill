Build a living inventory of appliances, warranties, and important home items in one place.

## When to use
Once to set up; ongoing to maintain. Especially useful at move-in/out or after appliance purchases.

## Why it matters
When an appliance breaks, you waste 30 minutes hunting warranty cards. When you move, you forget what you own. A home inventory fixes both.

## Steps
1. Walk each room with your phone. Photograph every appliance and valuable item.
2. Add each to the tracker template below (use Notes, Notion, or a spreadsheet).
3. Photograph warranty cards and insurance documents; attach to the relevant row or save to a folder named "Warranties".
4. Update when you buy, sell, or discard items.
5. Review once a year (good time: after Diwali / spring cleaning).

## Template
```
| Item | Brand/Model | Purchase date | Warranty expiry | Doc location | Replacement cost |
|------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|--------------|------------------|
| Washing machine | Samsung 7kg WW70 | 2023-11-10 | 2028-11-10 | Google Drive/Warranties | ₹28,000 |
| Laptop | Dell Inspiron 15 | 2022-06-01 | 2025-06-01 (expired) | — | ₹65,000 |
| Water purifier | Aquaguard | 2024-02-15 | 2026-02-15 | Kitchen drawer | ₹12,000 |
| TV | LG 43" | 2021-01-20 | Expired | — | ₹35,000 |
```

## Tips
- India context: extended warranties from Bajaj Finance, Croma, or manufacturer — check if they're registered online.
- Note the AMC (Annual Maintenance Contract) renewal date for ACs and water purifiers.
- A shared note with your partner or flatmate prevents duplicate purchases.
