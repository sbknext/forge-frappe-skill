Design a morning routine you'll actually stick to by starting small and building up over 30 days.

## When to use
Whenever you want to redesign your mornings — after a job change, move, or when current mornings feel chaotic.

## Framework
Morning routines fail because people try to do too much. Start with a 20-minute "minimum viable morning" and only expand after 30 days of consistency.

## Steps
1. Define your non-negotiable: what is the ONE thing that, if done before work, sets your day right? (Examples: exercise, journaling, reading, prayer, meditation.)
2. Decide your wake-up window — not a single time, but a 30-minute window (e.g. 6:00–6:30).
3. Design a 20-minute sequence: anchor non-negotiable + 2 hygiene steps.
4. Run it for 30 days. Track ✓/✗ daily.
5. After 30 days of >80% success, add one more element (10 min max).

```

Wk3: M T W T F S S

```

## Tips
- Lay out everything the night before — clothes, water, charger. Decision fatigue is the enemy of morning consistency.
- Protect your first 20 minutes from phone notifications. Airplane mode or Do Not Disturb.
- If you miss a day, do a 5-minute version rather than nothing — the streak psychology is less important than the identity reinforcement.
