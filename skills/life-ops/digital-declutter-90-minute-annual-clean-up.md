Systematically clear digital clutter across devices, cloud storage, and apps in one focused session.

## When to use
Once a year. Best paired with the subscription audit. Block 90 minutes.

## Session plan
```
Block 1 — 30 min: Files
[ ] Downloads folder: delete or file everything
[ ] Desktop: zero items remaining
[ ] Documents: archive anything older than 1 year into an Archive/YYYY folder
[ ] Delete duplicate photos (use free tool: Gemini 2 on Mac, Remo Duplicate on Windows)

Block 2 — 30 min: Apps + accounts
[ ] Uninstall apps not used in 90 days (phone + laptop)
[ ] Delete social media accounts you don't use
[ ] Remove saved passwords for sites you no longer use
[ ] Review browser bookmarks — delete dead links

Block 3 — 30 min: Email + cloud
[ ] Archive all email older than 6 months with one label/folder rule
[ ] Unsubscribe from newsletters (use Unroll.me or manual search for "unsubscribe")
[ ] Check Google Drive / iCloud storage — delete large unused files
[ ] Clear phone camera roll: backup to cloud, delete local copies of backed-up photos
```

## Tips
- Do this on a Saturday morning with no interruptions. Use a Pomodoro timer (25 min + 5 min break).
- Don't aim for perfection — the goal is 80% lighter, not zero clutter.
- After email, set up one rule: auto-archive (not inbox) for newsletters you want to read but not manage.
